The world is pre-seeded with a certain percentage of the cells populated by rocks, and a certain percentage populated by life. Of the cells populated by life, some percentage is populated by plants, and some by herbivores.

All life forms in the world have various common properties (which may take different values for plants and herbivores). These include the chance that in any given turn they will grow, produce offspring, or die, their maximum life span, how long after growing or generating offspring until they can do so again, and the number of offspring that they produce.

Herbivores have a specified starting energy, gain energy from eating plants (larger plants providing more energy) and will decrease in size if their energy drops to zero.
